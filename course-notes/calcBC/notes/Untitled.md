$$x^2+y^2=4$$
One way to differentiate this is to solve for $y$.

$$y=\pm\sqrt{4-x^2}$$
$$\frac{dy}{dx}=\pm\frac{1}{2\sqrt{4-x^2}}\cdot(-2x)=\mp\frac{x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}$$
$y$ is a function of $x$.

$$-\frac{x^2+y^2}{y^3}$$

$$\frac{d}{dx}x^2+\frac{d}{dy}y^2=0$$
$$\frac{d}{\cancel{dx}}x^2\cdot\frac{dx}{dy}+2y=0$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}x^2+2y=0$$
